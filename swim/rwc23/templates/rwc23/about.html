Rules

  1. You must be an open-water swimmer on Auckland North shore, or be associated with one. Basically if you know Jo, Alton, Jim, Geoff or Helen you are in.
  2. The game will be a series of mini-competitions, one a week. The first 5 are weeks 1 - 5 of the round robin competition. The next 3 will be the quarter finals, semi finals & then the final.
  3. $5 to enter each week. Based on feedback we may increase the entry to $10 for the last few weeks.
  4. Predictions must be provided via the Open Water swimming social WhatsApp group or Jim’s new website. Entries must be received a minimum of 15 minutes before kick-off.
  5. People who have paid (or pledged their entry fee), but forget to post their predicted score, or choose not to post a predicted score, will be assigned 0 points.

Scoring

  1. You must pick the winning team, or a draw, to get any points for that game.
  2. If you don't get your pick in before the game starts you don't get any points
  3. If you pick a draw you get 25 points (bonus for being brave).
  4. Your score is then based on points differential. The closer your selection is to the game score the more points you get..

Prizes

  • A coffee and a muffin (or equivalent) will be awarded to the winner of every round.
  • The remainder of the cash will accumulate until after the final, when a first, second, third and consolation prize is awarded, for the players with the lowest, second-lowest, third-lowest and highest points scores.

Judging

  • Meegan ‘the accountant’ Brewer has reviewed the points calculations. Jim ‘don’t call me honey’ West and Jo ‘Frau’ Hammer are administrators, and nominally the judges, and their decision is final. Recourse to legal and mediation services will be limited because the billable minutes will erode all gains.
  • The winner of each round is the person who first gets the actual result correct, then whose estimated score is closest to that of the winning team, in combination with the points difference (or spread). Getting close to the winning score is twice as important as getting close to the points difference.
